Chapter 1: The Broken Observatory

Lu Youqi arrived at the Moonfall Observatory just before winter dusk. The
stairs had cracked into silver dust, and every window faced the wrong
direction, as if the tower had turned away from the sky.

His teacher, Master Wen, gave him a brass astrolabe and warned him not to open
it. The astrolabe was warm even in the snow. Inside its glass face, three tiny
stars moved by themselves.

At midnight, Lu Youqi heard a voice from the sealed dome. It called him by his
childhood name, the one only his dead sister had used.

Chapter 2: The Girl With Ink-Black Eyes

The next morning, Lu Youqi found a girl sleeping inside the library fireplace.
She wore a travel cloak full of ash and carried a sword wrapped in white silk.

Her name was Shen Mian. She claimed she had crossed the northern desert to find
the Moon Gate, a hidden door that appeared only when three false stars aligned.
When Lu Youqi showed her the astrolabe, the stars inside it flashed blue.

Shen Mian said the astrolabe was not a tool. It was a key.

Chapter 3: The First False Star

Lu Youqi and Shen Mian climbed the eastern ridge to watch the sky. The first
false star appeared above the ruined bell tower, bright and motionless.

When Lu Youqi raised the astrolabe, the glass face cracked. A beam of blue
light struck the snow and revealed a buried staircase. The stairs led down to a
stone chamber filled with mirrors.

In the largest mirror, Lu Youqi saw himself older, crowned with pale fire. Shen
Mian saw nothing and refused to say why.

Chapter 4: Master Wen Lies

Master Wen admitted that he had known about the Moon Gate for thirty years. He
had once tried to open it with Lu Youqi's father, but the ritual failed and
destroyed the old city below the mountain.

He told Lu Youqi that the Moon Gate did not lead to heaven. It led to the
Court of Unwritten Gods, where forgotten spirits waited for human names.

Master Wen ordered him to destroy the astrolabe. Lu Youqi promised he would,
but hid it beneath the floorboards instead.

Chapter 5: The Bell That Rings Underwater

A bell rang beneath the frozen lake. Every person in the village heard it, but
only Lu Youqi could understand the sound. It repeated one sentence: return the
borrowed name.

Shen Mian confessed that her sword contained a spirit named Bai Xue, who had
once served the Court of Unwritten Gods. Bai Xue said Lu Youqi's family had
stolen a divine name generations ago.

That night, Master Wen disappeared. On his desk, Lu Youqi found a map marked
with three places: the lake, the old city, and the Moon Gate.

Chapter 6: Descent Into the Old City

Lu Youqi and Shen Mian entered the tunnels below the observatory. The old city
was not empty. Paper lanterns floated through the streets, each one carrying a
memory from someone who had died there.

In the market square, Lu Youqi found a statue of his father kneeling before a
door of black jade. The statue held a cracked tablet that named Lu Youqi as the
last heir of the borrowed god-name.

Shen Mian wanted to leave, but Lu Youqi insisted they continue.

Chapter 7: The Second False Star

The second false star appeared inside the underground sky painted on the
ceiling of the old palace. It shone red instead of blue.

When Lu Youqi touched it with the astrolabe, the palace walls unfolded like
paper. Behind them stood Master Wen, alive but changed. His hair had turned
white, and his shadow moved separately from his body.

Master Wen said he had made a bargain. To save Lu Youqi, he had promised Shen
Mian's sword-spirit a human heart.

Chapter 8: Shen Mian's Secret

Shen Mian revealed that she was not fully human. Her mother had been a temple
guardian, and her father had been one of the nameless spirits beyond the Moon
Gate.

She had come to the observatory because the Court of Unwritten Gods was calling
her home. If the gate opened, she would have to choose between becoming a god's
servant or remaining mortal and losing her memories.

Lu Youqi swore he would find another way. Shen Mian did not believe him, but
she smiled for the first time.

Chapter 9: The City Remembers Fire

The old city woke during the new moon. Streets repaired themselves, doors
opened, and ghosts stepped out carrying bowls of flame.

A ghost child led Lu Youqi to the Hall of Names. There, thousands of tablets
hung from red cords. One tablet bore his sister's childhood name. Another bore
his own name, already half-erased.

Lu Youqi realized the voice in the observatory had not been his sister. It was
the borrowed god-name wearing her memory like a mask.

Chapter 10: The Trial of the Three Names

At dawn, the third false star appeared inside the astrolabe itself. The brass
case opened, revealing three names written in light: Lu Youqi, Shen Mian, and
Yuehen, the forgotten moon god.

The Court demanded a trial. Lu Youqi could keep his life, save Shen Mian, or
return the stolen god-name, but he could not choose all three.

Master Wen begged him to choose life. Shen Mian begged him to choose the truth.
Lu Youqi chose the god-name.

Chapter 11: The Moon Gate Opens

The Moon Gate opened beneath the observatory dome, not in the old city. Black
jade unfolded into a circle of white flame, and the sky above the tower split
like wet paper.

Yuehen stepped through wearing Lu Youqi's face. The forgotten moon god thanked
him for returning what his ancestors had stolen.

But Lu Youqi refused to surrender completely. He asked Yuehen to spare Shen
Mian and the village. In exchange, he offered every memory he had of his own
childhood.

Chapter 12: The Price of Mercy

Yuehen accepted the bargain. The village survived, and Shen Mian remained
mortal, but Lu Youqi forgot his sister's voice, his mother's face, and the
first poem Master Wen had taught him.

The astrolabe shattered into three pieces. One piece became a ring on Lu
Youqi's finger. One piece sank into the frozen lake. The last piece vanished
into Shen Mian's sword.

Master Wen died smiling, though his shadow continued to kneel beside the Moon
Gate.

Chapter 13: Ashes in the Spring Snow

Spring came late. Lu Youqi stayed at the observatory to repair the broken dome.
He wrote down every memory he still had, fearing the bargain would take more
from him while he slept.

Shen Mian prepared to leave for the southern capital. She said the sword-spirit
Bai Xue had grown quiet since the gate opened, but not silent.

Before leaving, Shen Mian gave Lu Youqi a paper charm and told him to burn it
only if the moon ever turned black.

Chapter 14: The Black Moon

Three months later, the moon turned black in the middle of the afternoon. Every
clock in the observatory stopped. The ring made from the astrolabe tightened
around Lu Youqi's finger until it drew blood.

When he burned Shen Mian's paper charm, her voice came through the flame. She
said Bai Xue had stolen the last astrolabe shard and was carrying it to the
southern capital.

Lu Youqi packed Master Wen's old map and left the mountain before sunset.

Chapter 15: A God With a Human Shadow

On the road south, Lu Youqi dreamed of Yuehen walking across a sea of black
glass. The moon god still wore Lu Youqi's face, but now he cast a human shadow.

In the dream, Yuehen said the bargain had not ended. By returning the god-name,
Lu Youqi had become its witness. If Yuehen was forgotten again, Lu Youqi would
vanish with him.

Lu Youqi woke with the ring glowing white. Ahead, the southern capital burned
under a moonless sky, and Shen Mian's sword sang from somewhere inside the
flames.
